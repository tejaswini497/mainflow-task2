Vegetables

Vegetables are a key part of a healthy diet. They provide essential nutrients and vitamins that contribute to overall health and well-being. Here are some of the most popular and nutritious vegetables:

Carrot

Carrots are rich in beta-carotene, fiber, vitamin K1, potassium, and antioxidants. They are known for their health benefits, including improved eye health and reduced risk of cancer.

Broccoli

Broccoli is a great source of vitamins K and C, folate (folic acid), potassium, and fiber. It also provides a good amount of protein compared to other vegetables.

Spinach

Spinach is loaded with nutrients and antioxidants. It is a rich source of vitamins A, C, and K, magnesium, iron, and manganese. It may benefit eye health, reduce oxidative stress, and help prevent cancer.

Tomato

Tomatoes are the major dietary source of the antioxidant lycopene, which has been linked to many health benefits, including reduced risk of heart disease and cancer. They are also a great source of vitamin C, potassium, folate, and vitamin K.
